    Spinach (Spinacia oleracea)

    Spinach (Spinacia oleracea) is a leafy green vegetable celebrated for its rich nutrient profile, including high levels of iron, calcium, and vitamins A, C, and K. Known for its dark green, tender leaves, spinach is a versatile ingredient that can be enjoyed raw in salads, sautéed, blended into smoothies, or added to a variety of dishes.

    Carrots (Daucus carota)

    Carrots (Daucus carota) are a popular root vegetable known for their vibrant orange color, though they also come in purple, yellow, white, and red varieties. Rich in beta-carotene, which the body converts to vitamin A, carrots are renowned for their benefits to eye health, skin, and immune function.

    Veggies

    Vegetables come in various forms, including leafy greens, root vegetables, cruciferous vegetables, legumes, and more. Each type of vegetable brings unique flavors, textures, and nutritional benefits. Cruciferous vegetables, such as broccoli and cauliflower, contain compounds that may reduce cancer risk.
